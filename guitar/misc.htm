Finding Minor/Major relation

To find the relative minor to a given major key do the following with your guitar: Say you want the relative minor to D, find the "d" on the g-string 3rd highest, it is on fret 7. Now look what note this fret, fret 7, would be on the (low or high) e-string, in this case fret 7 on the e-string is a B. Thus B is the reltive minor to D! Of course this works the other way round when trying to find the major to a known minor. All this is important when it comes to quickly know what fingering pattern (full or pentatonic) to use for soloing. Say you're jamming in Eminor, but you don't know where to put your standart pattern. Remember that the relative minors and majors have the same notes and therefore the same patterns. So, find the appropriate major key, G, which pattern you might know, because it is extremely widely used. Just remember that the root note is not G but E!
Anyway it's just a little thing I found some day....
